Music & Poetry

Beatnik Party

The Beats were very much influenced by jazz music and musicians. Kerouac would emulate bebop and cool jazz, and like other Beats, wanted his poetry and prose to have a similar musical language, rhythmic feeling and flow to what he heard in modern jazz. Who are the jazz musicians who influenced the Beats? Charlie Parker (aka “Bird”), who reinvented jazz music, established a new harmonic system, and blew everybody’s minds with his improvisations. There is Lennie Tristano, who devised a new way of playing piano, adapting Bird’s improvisations to the piano keyboard and influencing generations of other musicians. Saxophonist Warne Marsh was part of the Tristano school, and taught generations of young horn players the new style of blowing. Beat literature and bebop/cool jazz were both existential, outside the mainstream. It was in a way like outsider art, and the French understood it. The musicians mentioned here had lots in common with the Beat writers; they felt alienated from the mainstream music fans as well as from the racism of the day.
